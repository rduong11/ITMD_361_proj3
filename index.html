Hello, my name is Raymond Duong and I'm a 4th year CS major.

Where I'm From

I was born in raised here in Chicago! If you aren't from here, Chicago is a very neighborhood centered city.

My house is located in the West Ridge area, but I'm also bordered by Edgewater, Andersonville, and Rogers Park (more well-known neighborhoods).

Though I lived in this area, I primarily grew up in a neighborhood called Uptown, but I know it as Argyle.

Argyle

Argyle is a densely Vietnamese populated neighborhood.

From Broadway and Foster to Broadway and Ainslie, you will find a tightknit Vietnamese community, fostering:

  • Predominantly Vietnamese restaurants catering authentic and delicious food (most of the others are other Asian restaurants such as Chinese or Thai)
  • Stores that sell Vietnamese niche things, such as nail polish (Most Vietnamese immigrants are nail technicians), spices, and pottery
  • Vietnamese grocery markets that sell not just Vietnamese groceries but a lot of Asian/exotic groceries
  • Healthcare infrastructure that has Vietnamese-English speaking workers
  • Lots of boba restaurants/cafes; boba or bubble tea is a sweet drink that combines tea with milk and boba, which is basically tapioca pearls

Besides being surrounded by this community, I actually went to elementary school a couple blocks from here too.

From Pre-K through 8th grade, my parents enrolled me in a Catholic private school called St. Thomas of Canterbury.

Often times after school I would go to a summer camp called Vietnamese Association of Illinois, or go to my grandma's house a couple blocks down.

It's a little ironic that they enrolled me in this school, because my family practices Buddhism.

The story behind me going here is that they believed that a private school would have a more prestigious education/curriculum.

Though this was true, since it was a small private school, we lacked the resources that public schools had that were funded from the government directly.

I still remember using hand-me-down textbooks that were falling off their binder, and our school not being able to afford Chromebooks until I was around 5th or 6th grade... good times!

Nonetheless, to this day I am still so grateful for my parents and the sacrifices they've made for me to have a prosperous future.

So far, I think I'm doing good! It is my lifelong dream to be able to retire them and let them live comfortably. I am getting closer and closer to this goal as time goes on :]

High School

Skipping next in the education timeline, I attended a high school called Lane Tech College Prep.

High school was a pretty pivotal point in my life, allowing me to grow out of my shell and prosper socially and academically.

I didn't do many extracurriculars here, because I devoted most of my time to one club: Chinese Club.

Chinese Club

At my school, there were a lot of clubs, but by far the most populated were the ethnic clubs.

Every year around mid-March, Lane hosts an event called International Days/Nights.

It consists of all the ethnic clubs coming together to perform a choreographed ethnic dance that represents their culture.

Typically, clubs will start their tryouts for new members around October.

From this time until March, they will come up with choreography and practice, with practices ranging up to 1 hour to 3 hours depending on the proximity of the performance date.

It was at this school that I developed a passion for CS. We were fortunate enough to have lots of resources and amazing teachers for this career path.
